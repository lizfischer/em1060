Bilingual Rule of St. Benedict

Date: s. xi2

Summary:

This 'miscellaneous' manuscript (Catt. Vett., p. i), mainly written in s. xi2 with many additions in s. xii1, includes:

  • s. xviii, table of contents of manuscript (fol. [i]:1r)
  • s. xii, list of books read at collation (fol. [i]1v/1-11)
  • s. xii, inventory of about 450 books (fol. 1:2r/1a-2:3r/31b)
  • Letter of Pope Alexander III to Roger, archbishop of York and Hugh, Bishop of Durham, 26 July 1177 (fols 3:5v/1-33)
  • Kalendar (fols 8:8r/1-11v/33)
  • Durham Martyrology (fols 12:14v/1-39r/38)
  • Gospel Capitulla (fols 39v/1-45r/14)
  • Lanfranc's Constitutions (ff. 47r/1-71v/38)
The Old English is found in the bilingual version of the Rule of St. Benedict. Æthelwold's Old English translation (fols 98v/1-123v/28) follows the Latin text (fols 74v-95v). This arrangement is 'different from that of all other bilingual copies of the Rule, in which the Old English follows the Latin chapter by chapter' (Ker, p. 148). Although the Old English version has a very similar layout to the Latin version, it may be have been written 'as an afterthought' as Piper suggests ('Durham Cantor's Book', pp. 80-81).

The capitula of the Latin text are numbered I- LXXIII, but the chapters in the text itself are numbered I- LXXII, because chapter 62 is omitted. The Old English has no capitula, chapter-headings, or chapter numbers, but the text is written in paragraphs which agree with the chapters of the Latin text, and each paragraph begins with the first words of the corresponding Latin chapter. Unlike the Latin text, chapter 62 is not omitted (Ker, p. 148).

Physical Description:

Object Description:

    Extent:

      ca. 265 mm x ca. 180 mm (dimensions of all - size of page)
      ca. 200 mm x ca. 130 mm (dimensions of all - size of written space)

    The manuscript is 'a compendium of five discrete sets of quires which broadly correspond to contents' (Keefer, p. 101).

    • quire I: Various added texts and records
    • quires II-V (+VI?): The calendar, Usuard, and Gospel capitula
    • quires VII-X: Lanfranc
    • quires XI-XIII: Latin Rule of St. Benedict
    • quires XIV-XVII: Old English Rule of St. Benedict

    Rule of St. Benect is in six quires of eight leaves (fols 74-121), followed by one quire of six leaves (fols 122-27, it lacks original 5, and now with a singleton in its place). The four quires containing the Old English text (fols 98-127) have their own signatures, I-iii, at the end.

Hand Description:

    Number of hands: 1 for Old English
    Summary: The Old English is contemporary with the Latin text of the Rule, but 'in a better hand' (Ker, p. 149).
    Hand: The Old English Rule of St. Benedict (fols 98-127)
    • Scope: Major
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand: Palaeographical images can be found in the C11 Database Project. Ker lists the following characteristics of this hand:
    • ð, not þ, is used for th.
    • descenders are short with ends curving to the left.
    • Abbreviations:
    • The nota ˥ is raised above the general level of the letters.

Decoration Description:

    The initials, alternately purple (sometimes filled with yellow) and red, are by the same hand throughout (fols 74-127) (Ker, p. 149).

Binding Description:

    19th century binding (Ker, p. 149).

History:

    Origin:

    Unknown. Keeefer suggests that Rule of St. Benedict has 'a form better suited to a Canterbury origin in the 1080s than it does to Durham Cathedral Priory' (p. 99).

    Provenance:

    According to Catt. Vett. (p. 30), Rule of St. Benedict (fols 74-127) were at Durham by s. xi/xii and were bound with fols 1-73 at least by 1391.

    Ker suggests that they were bound together much earlier, as the whole manuscript is likely to be the 'Martyrologium et Regula' given to Durahm by Bishop William of St. Carilef (d. 1096). See Catt. Vett., p. 118. The Old English text is styled 'regula Sancti benedicti in gallico' by a late 15th century cataloguer of the Durham Cathedral Library (fol. 12).
