Life of St. Guthlac

Date: ( [])

Summary:

A translation of parts of the pentateuch and joshua, partly at least by Ǽlfric, followed by Ǽlfric's homily on judges, his letter to Wulfgeat, and his Libellus de veteri testamento et nouo adressed to Sigeweard. At the end is a life of St. Guthlac. Arts 1, 2, 4 coll. and partly pr. Crawford 1922, who refers to this copy as L. Latin glosses have been added on many leaves in a hand of s. xi/xii (see fascism): among them is the English word 'bǽcelinge' (g insular), written above 'coctum in clibano' which itself glosses the OE 'ofebacene hlafas' on fol. 66v (lev. 24). Running titles and marginalia in Latin are in pencil and ink, s. xiii/xiv: the writer marked omisions in the translation of the biblical books by the word 'oblitus'. William Lisle wrote notes in the margins of Laud, fol. 24rv, altered the text occasionally, underlined it, and collated it with no. 142 in his Divers Ancient Monuments (1623).

History:

    Origin:
    Provenance:
    Acquisition: The first 2 leaves of quire 18 are in Oxford (fl. 140-1) and the rest of it on London. 'No. 159' in the old Royal Library (fol. 2, top), in the catalogue of which, compiled in 1542, it is described as 'the pentatuik and saints Lyues'. Belonged later to cotton (see N. R. Ker. in Brit. Mus. Quarterly, xii (1938), 132), who lent the part now at Oxford, 'Liber Genesis et pentatuchum Saxonice bound withe my arms and clapses in 4to' to 'Mr. Lyll of Cambrig' before 32rd of April 1621, as appears from the list of Cotton's loan of books in MS. Harley 6018, fol. 148v. The part of the manuscript in Oxford stlll has its Cottonian table of contents (fol. iv). Extracts copied from it when it was in the Cotton collection are in Boldeleian MS. James 18 (3855), fol. 66. t is no. 81 and Vesp. is part of no. 80 in the 1621Cottonian catalogue in Harley 6018. Laud bears Archbishop Laud's inscription of ownership, dated 1638, on fol. 1 and was given by him to the Boldeleian in 1639. The dates of ownership and gift suggest that Laud obtained the manuscript from Lisle (cf. Laud misc. 201, 381, 636). Former Boldeleian pressmark Laud E. 19. Wanley, p. 67.
