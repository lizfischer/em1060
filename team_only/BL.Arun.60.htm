Date: s. xi2, 1099

Summary:

Manuscript Items:

  1. Item (i):

      Title (standard):

      Title (manuscript):

      () ()

      Text Language:

      Other versions of the text:

      Continuous interlinear gloss to the psalms (ff. 13-46v, 53-119) and canticles (ff.119-30) of the Gallican version. The psalms and canticles are preceded by a calendar (Wormald 1934, no. 11) and computus tables (ff. 1-12) and are followed by a litany (Wormald, Anal. Boll. lxiv (1946), 76) and Latin prayers (ff. 130v-48v). Ff. 133-42, two quires, were added in s. xii. The psalms are numbered in red. Ps. 151 occurs, but without gloss. The canticles are in Mearns order, 1-7, 9, 8, 11, 10, 12-15 (J. Mearns, Canticles (1914), p. 65).

      Title (standard):

      Title (manuscript):

      () Miserere mei deus secundum magnam misericordiam tuam ()

      Text Language:

      Other versions of the text:

      Gloss of a long prayer in Latin based on the phrases of Psalm 50. The last leaf, f. 149, contains two additions, written apparently in 1099, in a hand which does not occur elsewhere.
