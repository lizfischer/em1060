History:

    Origin: This copy of the OE version of the Gospels dates from the 12c/13c and is a direct copy of London, British Library, Royal 1. A. xiv (280), a Canterburymanuscript; palaegraphical evidenceals suggests a Canterbury origin. Later medieval history is unknown. The manuscript belinged to John Parker (1548-1618), son of Archbishop Mathew Parker(his signiture may be seen on the verso of fol. i) and Christopher, Baron Hatton(1605?-1670), whose signature is on the recto of fol. ii; used by Francis Junius (1589-1677) for his1665 edition of the Gospel.
    Provenance:
    Acquisition: Aqcuired by the Bodleian Library with other Hatton manuscripts in 1671.
