Bilingual Rule of Chrodegang

Date: s. xi 3/4

Summary:

A bilingual copy, in Latin and English, of Chrodegang of Metz's Regula canonicorum, written at Exeter in the 11th century, from an exemplar from Winchester. Each Latin chapter of the text is followed by an English translation.

Manuscript Items:

  1. Item : fols 1r/1-86r/17

      Title (standard): Chrodegang of Metz, Regula Canonicorum

      Title (manuscript): (1r/1-2) Title added at top margin in later hand reads: De ordine canonicorum. Martilogium. liber utilis exceptis omnibus expositionibus in anglico

      Incipit (Latin): (1r/4-6) SI TRECENTORUM DECEM ET | OCTO RELIQUORUM SANCTORUM PATRVM | canonum auctoritas inuiolata.

      Explicit: (86r/15-17) þa ageat oƿenlice ....... | ..arn lea sa ceorl hƿa.. he und.. f...g. oððe | hƿæne se biscop ut hæfde adraƿed.

      Text Language: Text is Latin; each chapter is followed by a translation into English.

      Other versions of the text:

      • Cambridge, Corpus Christi College, 190; Ker 45B article 7
      • London, British Library, Add. 34652, f. 3; Ker 128

      Bibliography:

Physical Description:

Object Description:

Form: codex

Support: The MS was written on leaves of parchment of rough preparation. Timothy Graham notes that the "brownish" leaves are "often grainy and scaly" and remarks upon the number of original holes present in the volume. Leaf ordering is HFHF (ASMMF 11.3).

Extent: fols 1r/1-86r/17 xv + 1 + i + 1 + ii + 82 + xii. Endleaves are 20th century paper and 16th century vellum (ASMMF 11.3). Dimensions given below are approximate for some leaves due to subsequent damage.

    288 mm x 177 mm (; size of leaves)
    223 mm x 102 mm (; size of written)

Foliation and/or Pagination: Pagination written in upper right corner of verso leaves in Parkerian red crayon.

Collation:

    Quires: 1 (8), wants 2, replaced by 16th century leaf with note, 16th century repair strips containing fragments of 3 and 4, now pasted to 20th century leaves, inserted between 3 and 4; 2-6 (8); 7 (12), lacks 4, 5, 7 and 10; 8-10 (8); 11 (8), wants 6, 7 and 8.
    Signatures: No signatures.
    Catchwords: No catchwords.

Condition: The first quire of the manuscript has been extensively damaged. Leaves in this quire have suffered rotting and staining (Graham, ASMMF 11.3); these leaves are now uneven and rough at the edges. Some text from the preface and table of chapters has been lost, and many repairs of both the 16th and 20th centuries are evident. 1(2) is missing altogether, and has been since the 16th century; Parker notes on an inserted leaf that the Latin list of chapters is missing. Other leaves in this quire survive only as small fragments. Subsequent quires are in substantially better condition, with the physical structure of all leaves intact; however, the ink used is faded in places, making legibility difficult, and pigments used for rubrication of chapter titles and initials have corroded.

Layout description:
  1. Layout:
    • Columns: 1
    • Written Lines: 27
    • Locus: fols 1r/1-86r/17
    • Overview: Drypoint ruled throughout in one column of 27 lines. Pricking occurs only in the outer margin of each leaf, visible in quires 1-4; pricking in other quires has been trimmed off. The hair sides are ruled, with "pairs of vertical bounding lines at each side of the column" and two horizontal rule-lines each at top and bottom of each leaf extending across the page, or to the prick on quires where pricks are still present (Graham, ASMMF 11.3).

Accompanying Material:

History:

    Origin: Written by a scribe known to have been active at Exeter during the Episcopate of Leofric (1050-1072) (Graham, ASMMF 11.3). The MS is believed to be the "regula canonicorum" listed in a document cataloguing the material Leofric provided for Exeter, copies of which list are included in the Exeter book and in Oxford, Bodeian Auct. D. 2. 16. (Graham, ASMMF 11.3; Ker 46, pp. 74-5).
    Provenance: At Exeter until at least s. xiv 3/4, evidenced by notes written by John of Grandisson, bishop of Exeter until 1369. Bound at one time with CCCC 201 and CCCC 196 (Graham, ASMMF 11.3).
    Acquisition: Acquired by Parker during his term as archbishop of Canterbury (Graham, ASMMF 11.3).
