Homilies and lives of saints

Date: s. xi1-s. xi2

Summary:

A collection of seven folios from binding containing fragments of homilies and lives of saints, including Ælfric's texts. Fols 1-3v: Ælfric'sLife of St. Swithun, '7 oferworht... Þa sǽde se' (fols 1, 2) and 'hwæt...sibbe' (fol. 3); fols. 4-6v a fragment of Life of St. Mary of Egypt, '[...]s...adune as[....]' (fol. 4r), 'weardre ...þa þa' (fol. 5r), 'wæs genyrwed... ende. Amen' (f. 6r); fol. 6v The Rule of St. Benedict, chapter 4. It begins '[Æ]rest mon sceal god lufian of ealre heortan' and ends: 'on criste. þonne'; fol 7r1-3 contains three incomplete lines of the end of Ælfric's Passio apostolorum Petri et Pauli: '[...]uð is geond ...[.]y wuldor'; fol. 7r6-7v contains fifty-five lines of the beginning of Ælfric's In festiuitate Sancti Petri by : '[...]ge þæt ... andwear[d]an'. On fol. 7v, a later hand, s. xi2 added in the margin: 'Heo soðlice mid worde gefæstnode þæt hit swa wære. 7'. This is a translation of Acts 12: 15 added next to 'engel'. (See, Ker 1957, Art. 1-5.)

Physical Description:

Object Description:

    Seven half-sheets. According to Ker (1957), fols 1, 2 were probably adjacent. She also argues that 'probably three leaves separated f. 2 from f. 3. Two leaves separated f. 4 from f. 5 and four leaves separated f. 5 from f. 6.' (Ker 1957, p. 154). The folios have uneven measures: fol. 6 is 294 x 223 mm, and fol. 7r is 273 x 94 mm.

    Written space 269 mm. x 165 mm., and the text is wrtten on 60 long lines

    Ruling is visible on the rectos of fols. 1, 5 and the versos of 2-4, 6.

Hand Description:

    Number of hands: 3
    Summary: These fragments are written by at least three hands. Ker notes that fols. 1-3 and 7 are copied in a hand of s. xi1. A hand of s. ximed copied fols. 4-6v. Fol. 6v is completed by another hand of s. xi2 (Ker 1957, p. 154).
    Hand: main text
    • Scope: major
    • Scribe: Ker 117 Gneuss 262
    • Script: Insular
    • Description: fols 1-3, 7
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand:
    • aof squarish proportion.
    • e with a raised head in clusters such as æg and æt
    • y 'the left stroke rises higher than the right'.
    Hand: Amain text
    • Scope: major
    • Scribe: Ker 117 Gneuss 262
    • Script: Insular
    • Description: fols. 4- 6v Round, close-set hand (Ker 1957, p. 154).
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand:
    • e prominently horned, with a raised head in clusters such as æ.
    • descenders: the ends of the descenders curve to the left.
    Hand: main text
    • Scope: major
    • Scribe: Ker 117 Gneuss 262
    • Script: English Vernacular Minuscle
    • Description: fol. 6v A later hand.
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand:
    • ð curved cross-bared ascender 'which touches, but does not transect, the back'.

History:

    Origin:

    Unknown

    Provenance:

    Fols 1-3 were found in Register of Abbots Braunche (1500-10) and Newton (1510-14). Fols. 1,3 were pastedowns which left offset of writing on the binding boards. Fol. 2 was a flyleaf. Fols. 5 - 6 were pastedowns in another unknown volume (Ker 1957, p. 155).
