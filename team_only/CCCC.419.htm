Homilies

Date: s. xiI

Summary:

A collection of homilies, mostly by Ælfric and Wulfstan, written in late West-Saxon; many of the homilies are unique to this manuscript (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).

Physical Description:

Object Description:

Form: codex

Support: Parchment is finely-prepared throughout, although several leaves have tears evidently present before the text was written. Leaf arrangement, per Wilcox, is HFHF (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).

Extent: fols 1r/1-183v/19 iv + 184 + iv. The 4 leaves at either end of the manuscript each consist of one parchment and one paper bifolia, all 16th c. (ASMMF 8.1).

    205 mm x 130 mm (dimensions of all - size of leaves)
    175 mm x 90 mm (dimensions of all - size of written)

Foliation and/or Pagination:

Parker's pagination, at top right of recto of leaves, in red pencil. 366 pages. The first folio, illustrated, is unnumbered; numbering begins at one on the second leaf.

Collation:

    Quires: 1 (8), 1 removed and moved to front of quire; 3, 6 are singleton; 2-9 (8); 10 (8), 4,5 are singletons; 11 (8), 4, 5 are singletons; 12-15 (8); 16 (8), 2, 7 are singletons; 12-20 (8); 21 (8), 3, 6 are singletons; 22 (8), 2, 7 are singletons; 23 (8), 2, 7 are singletons (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).
    Signatures: No signatures.
    Catchwords: No catchwords.

Condition:

The manuscript is generally in very good condition. Occasional tears originating from the time of production exist, but subsequent damage is limited to fading of ink which sometimes impedes legibility, particularly toward the end of the book. Light staining is present throughout.

Layout description:
  1. Layout:
    • Columns: 1
    • Written Lines: 19
    • Locus: fols 1r/1-183v/19
    • Overview: Drypoint ruled, 19 lines per page. Top and bottom lines "extend across the page", as do "double bounding lines". Some pricking visible. (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).

Hand description:

    Number of hands: 1

This scribe also partly responsible for Corpus Christi College 421 (Ker 69). Wilcox notes that this scribe was a "carefu[l] corrector" of his work (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).

    Methods of Correction: For a detailed account of scribal correction of this manuscript, see Wilcox 1988, pp. 21-37.
    • Scope: sole
    • Script: Anglo-Saxon Insular Minuscule
    • Description: fols 1r/1-183v/19 A very round Anglo-Saxon insular minuscule hand "of the first half of the eleventh c." (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).
    • Language: Wilcox (ASMMF 8.1) notes that the language is "mostly standard late-West Saxon but with considerable tolerance for non-standard forms", citing Wilcox 1988, pp. 196-221.

Decoration Description:

    The present first leaf, unnumbered, contains "a drawing of Christ's entry into Jerusalem, taken from a 13[th c.] psalter, and replacing a first folio now bound with CCCC 421." Christ is "painted in bright colors," his clothes are "blue and red; a tree, the ground, and Christ's halo are green; and the ass and people's skin are white on a gold background." Illustrations from this psalter "now [also] appear in two other 11[th c.] manuscripts which passed through Parker's hands, Lambeth Place 771 and CCCC 452" (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).

Binding Description:

    Last rebound in November 1954, preserving an 18th c. binding (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).

History:

    Origin:

    Wilcox (ASMMF 8.1) suggests that although the contents of this manuscript and the closely-related CCCC 421 show "textual affiliation with south eastern manuscripts, most fully Cambridge, Trinity College B. 15.34, which was written at Christ Church, Canterbury) and that the language is "most close to south eastern manuscripts, especially those from Canterbury, the "idiosyncratic content of the two [i.e., the present MS and CCCC 421] manuscripts ... with their high number of unique anonymous homilies militates against a Canterbury origin ... Instead, the range of contents suggests that these manuscripts were compiled in a scriptorium less central to the mainstream of homiletic tradition than those of Canterbury ... That scriptorium may be the unknown center to which Trinity B. 15. 34 traveled after it was written at Canterbury, since the two manuscripts share some corrections."

    Provenance:

    Owned by Archbishop Parker in the 16th century, when "it was probably ... rebound. the original opening folio transferred to CCCC 421, and the current opening folio added" (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).

    Acquisition:

    The manuscript was given to Corpus Christi College by Parker upon his death (Wilcox, ASMMF 8.1).
