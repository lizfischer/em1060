Bede's Death Song

Date: s. xii1

Summary:

A composite manuscript, which contains items copied in various centuries (s. xii1-s. xv). Items from s. xii1 are Symeon of Durham, 'Liber de exordio atque procursu Dunelmensis ecclesiae' (fols 25r-97r), Pseudo-Bede, 'De Quindecim Signis' (fols 99r-99v) and Pseudo-Augustine 'De Antichristo quomodo et ubi nasci debeat' (fols 99v-102r).

'Bede's Death Song' in OE (West Saxon Version) is part of Symeon of Durham's 'Liber', and appears in fol. 43r/7-11.

The collection was originally made up of this manuscript and Dublin, Trinity College 114, which contains Clement of Llanthony (s. xivmed) and Sermons (s. xvmed).

Physical Description:

Object Description:

    Fols ii + 104 + v; parchment except for the paper endleaves. According to Lucas, 'pricking done with a wheel in Quires III-IX (fols. 25-97) is visible on the outer edge of many leaves' (p. 43). The ruling scheme is very similar to that in Durham, University Library Cosin V. II. 6 (124), which contains corrections by Symeon of Durham (Gullick).

    'Bede's Death Song' is 'written out like prose with pointing at the end of verse-lines' (Lucas, p. 44).

Hand Description:

    Number of hands: 1 for Old English
    Hand: Main hand
    • Scope: Major
    • Script: English Vernacular Miniscule for English
    • Description: According to Ker (pp. 189-90), 'Bede's Death Song' (fol. 43r/7-11) was written by the main hand who copied entire Symeon of Durham's 'Liber de exordio atque procursu Dunelmensis ecclesiae' (fols 25r-97r), but he used 'the special insular letter-forms' for 'Bede's Death Song' (fol. 43r/7-11).
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand: Ker and Lucas discuss the hand of 'Death Song' and give characteristics of the following letters:
    • a in 'gehiggene' is in Caroline form;
    • d is in Insular form; d and ð are 'of the same shape and size';
    • e in 'gehiggene' and the first 'heonen' are in Caroline form;
    • f is in Insular form;
    • g is in Insular form;
    • h in 'gehiggene' and the first 'heonen' are in Caroline form;
    • r is in Insular form;
    • s in 'ƿances' is in Caroline form;
    • þ is in Insular form;
    • ð is in Insular form;
    • y is in Insular form;
    • the end of descenders turn to the left;

History:

    Origin:

    Symeon of Durham's 'Liber', which includes 'Bede's Death Song' was originally written at Durham. It is 'an early witness of Symeon of Durham's 'Historia Dunelmensis ecclesiae' (Lucas, p. 42)

    Provenance:

    The manuscript was originally together with Dublin, Trinity College 114. Henry Savile the Elder (1547-1622) dismembered and gave this to Thomas Allen (1542-1632) of Oxford in 1589. Allen, then gave it to Cotton (Allen's promise to Cotton is recorded in BL Harley 6018, fol. 150v, dated as 30 April 1621). See Lucas, p. 42.
