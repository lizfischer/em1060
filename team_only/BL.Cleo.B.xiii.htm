History:

    Origin: The script, the use of c-shaped accents, the format and the number of lines to the page associate this manuscript with nos. 68, 69, and especially 283, q.v.: like them it is almost certainly from Exeter.
    Provenance: The provenance was known to Richard James in s. xvii1, if, as seems almost certain, his note in Bodleian MS. James 27 (Sum. Cat. 3864), p. 91, 'Here is se geleafa 7 gebed 7 bletsung lǽwedum mannum. ðe þǽt leden ne cunnnon in codice Exon. bibliothecǽ', refers to this manuscript, art. 10.
    Acquisition: It cannot have been actually at Exeter as late as this, since it was used by the archbishop Parker and bears the signature of his son John Parker on fol. 2 in red pencil. Presumably James was copying from a sixteenth century transcript, perhaps one of Joscelyn's. Belonged to cotton in 1621: Harley 6018, no. 70. Wanley, p. 201.
