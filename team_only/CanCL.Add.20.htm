Bilingual Rule of Chrodegang (fragment)

Date: s. xi2

Summary:

A fragment of the Latin-Old English recension of the Rule of Chrodegang, containing parts of chapters 39, 40 and 43. There is a complete copy in Cambridge, CCC 191, and part of chapter 61 in London, BL, Add. 34652.

Physical Description:

Object Description:

    A fragment of a bifolium. According to Ker, it 'formed the wrapper of a small book' (p. 139). Initial in scarlet, outlined in pale pink, Rubrics in pale orange (Doane, 1997, p. 1).

Hand Description:

    Number of hands: 1
    Hand: Main
    • Scope: Major
    • Script: Insular Miniscule for English and Anglo-Caroline for Latin
    • Description: According to Doane, 1997, 'a round Anglo-Caroline in the Latin and an Insular minuscule of similar character in the OE' (p. 1). Ker describes it as 'a large round hand' (p. 139). C11 Database Project describes the hand and includes sample images.
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand: Ker gives characteristics of the following letters:
    • e in EO is round-backed (as in Latin);
    • long and high forms of s are used indifferently;
    • rounded y in Latin, and straight-limbed y in OE, both forms are dotted.

History:

    Provenance:

    Ker identifies it as 'Regula canonicorium' among the 'Libri anglici' in the early 14th century catalogue of Christ Church, Canterbury, but James says that the entry is presumably to 'the Rule of St. Augustine' (See Doane, 1997, p. 1).

    Acquisition:

    Formerly 'Box CCC no. xixa'. It was discovered by William Urry in the 1940s in Canterbury (Ker, p. vii).
