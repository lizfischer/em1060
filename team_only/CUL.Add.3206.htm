Ecclesiastical Law

Date: s. xi2

Summary:

A fragment of two adjacent leaves, containing part of Ecclesiastical Law, the same text as fols 22v/8 - 26/13 of Junius 121. Possibly from South West. Some lines are missing on each leaf and letters in the inner margin of the second leaf.

Physical Description:

Object Description:

Form: Fragment

Support: Parchment. Two adjacent leaves.

Extent:

    160 mm x 118 mm (dimensions of first leaf - size of leaf)
    151 mm x 108 mm (dimensions of first leaf - size of written space)
    162 mm x 108 mm (dimensions of second leaf - size of leaf)
    162 mm x 98 mm (dimensions of second leaf - size of written space)

Foliation and/or Pagination: Paginated 1-4.

Collation:

Some lines are missing on each leaf and letters in the inner margin of the second leaf. Probably five lines are missing from the top of the first leaf and three lines from the top of the second leaf.

Condition:

Surface of the parchments are damaged from the glue used for the binding. There are also some holes.

Layout description:

  1. Layout:
    • Layout type: unknown
    • Columns: 1
    • Written Lines: 25
    • Locus: The first leaf
    • Overview: The layout type and dimensions of ruled area are unknown due to the loss of part of the leaf. Probably it originally had 30 lines. Ruled with stylus. The outer boundary is visible on p. 1.
  2. Layout:
    • Layout type: unknown
    • Columns: 1
    • Written Lines: 27
    • Locus: The second leaf
    • Overview: The layout type and dimensions of ruled area are unknown due to the loss of part of the leaf. Probably it originally had 30 lines. 26 lines and one-third of the 27th line are visible. The outer boundary is visible on p. 4.

Hand description:

    Number of hands: 1
    Summary: Both leaves (Items 1, 2, 3 and 4) were written by the same scribe.
    Methods of Correction: Letters are added interlinearly, and insertion points are marked by caret marks in the shape of a virgule.
    Hand: 1
    • Scope: major
    • Script: Anglo-Saxon Insular miniscule
    • Description: The aspect is broad but pointed and slightly backward-leaning. Ascenders are often split or wedged and slightly taller than the height of the body. Straight descenders are as long as the height of the body, or sometimes shorter, and have upward serif to the right. Larger tironian marks are used at the beginning of sentences, and Rustic capitals at the beginning of a section.
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand:
    • Insular single compartment a is triangular, but rounded;
    • a of æ is rounded triangular, and the same height as or slightly higher than e;
    • Insular d is round-backed, with the short upstroke at 45', which tapers backward to the right;
    • e is round and its horizontal line goes up to the right, and often finishes with a tongue;
    • Insular f: the downstroke is straight with a wedged top and finishes with a serif that turns up to the right, and its tongue is longer than the shoulder;
    • Insular g has a flat top; The vertical stroke joins the horizontal bar towards the left, and the lower lobe is closed. The sizes or the upper and lower lobes are the same;
    • Insular h: the ascender with a split top; both limbs are straight and have distinct serifs that turn up right at 45';
    • minims have distinct serifs that turn up right at 45';
    • two kinds of s: Insular s with a straight descender with a serif to the right; and a long s with a descender which descends slightly below the writing line. The descender of longs tappers slightly to the left at the end;
    • t is round-backed, and its head stroke is horizontal. The back stroke joins the head stroke at the left end;
    • the ascender of ð is tall at 45', and tapers slightly backward to the right. The prominent crossbar has an upward serif to the right at its left end, and downward serif to the left at its right end;
    • the ascender of þ is wedged or has a split, and its descender is short and has a serif to the right;
    • the descender of ƿ is straight and finishes with an upward serif to the right;
    • y is dotted, the first stroke is thick, whereas the second stroke is a thin line with a wedge of a split at the top, and its descender finishes with a upward serif to the right;
    • ascenders are often split, or wedged at the top (see b, l, þ);
    • descenders are straight and often finish with distinct serifs that turns up to the right at 45' (see Insular low r, low s, ƿ).
    • Abbreviations:
    • the crossbar of þæt has an upward serif to the right at its left end, and downward serif to the left at its right end;
    • macron has an upward serif to the right at its left end, and downward serif to the left at its right end;
    • the descender of ˥ is straight and usually has a serif to the right at the end. Its head slightly caves in. Sometimes a larger ˥ is used at the beginning of sentences.
    • Punctuation: · is used.

Decoration Description:

    The beginning of Item (5), 'R' is in red.

    Some capital letters are in black, and coloured in red.

Additions:

    There is no additional material.

Binding Description:

    The fragment is now kept in a black loose cover.

Accompanying Material:

    A fragment of printed book, which was probably also used for the binding, is attached to the second leaf (pp. 3-4). Some letters of the printed fragment are visible on the right hand side of p. 3.

    A note in the handwriting of Dr Felix Liebermann, which explains the contents of the leaves, is kept with the fragments.

    Another note singed by 'The Librarian University Library' contains the information about the most recent sale. It says: 'Add. 3206 Saxon Homilies. Ab. 1050 two leaves. 1893. Sotheby's Bateman Sale lot. 1528'.

History:

    Provenance:

    The leaves were pastedowns in the binding of a book of octavo size. Formerly part of CUL Additional 4166, a collection of fragments bought by s. Sandars in the Bateman sale at Sotheby's, 25 May 1893, lot 1528.

    Acquisition:

    Bequeathed by Sandars in 1894.
