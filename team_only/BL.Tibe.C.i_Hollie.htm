Computistica; Astronomica; Pontifical; Homilies

Date: s.xi med- s. xi/xii

Summary:

A composite manuscript made up of two distinct parts, which were joined together under the direction of Robert Cotton (Ker 1959, p. 262).

Part 1 (ff. 2-42) is in turn made up of two parts: Part 1a (ff. 2-17), written in Peterborough between 1122 and 1135, originally formed part of a scientific manuscript belonging with London, BL, Harley 3667, as indicated by the similarities of script and quire signatures (Wilcox 2000), p. 30). Part 1b (ff. 18-42), probably also produced at Peterborough in the 1120s (Ker 1938, p. 32) contain illustrated astronomical material.

Part 2 (ff. 43- 203) contains a pontifical written by two scribes in Germany around s.ximed. Between 1070 and 1100, thirteen scribes writing in England added material on blank spaces and additional leaves ( Wilcox 2000, p. 30).

History:

    Origin:

    Part 1a was written in Peterborough between 1122 and 1135, perhaps around 1122. This is indicated by the annals in London, BL, Harley 3667 (Wilcox 2000, p. 69). Part 1b was probably produced in Peterborough in the 1120s, as is suggested by similarities in script and decoration (Ker 1938, p. 132).

    Part 2 was written in Germany probably in the middle of the 11th century (Ker 1959, p. 263).

    Provenance:

    Part 1a was probably still part of the larger manuscript in Peterborough in the late 14th century, as it is probably the book mentioned by James (1926, p. 34).

    Provenance of Parts 1b is unknown. Part 2 was added to in Sherborne and then Salisbury between 1070 and 1100. Quire XVIII contains the hand of an unlocalised scribe of the late 11th century.

    Acquisition:

    Probably bound together under the direction of Robert Cotton. Acquired by the British Museum along with the rest of the Cotton collection.
