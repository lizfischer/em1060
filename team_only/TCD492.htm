Bede's Death- Song

Date: s. xii med

Summary:

The manuscript has four items, mainly in Latin: Bede, 'Historia Ecclesiastica Gentis Anglorum' (fols 1r/1- 175v/5a), Cuthbert, 'Epistola De Obitu Bedae' (fols175v/6a-177r/4b), a short tract on the progress of Christianity in England (fols 177r/6b- 178v/9b) and 'Notationes De Sanctis qui in Anglia Requiescunt' (fols 178v/11b- 180r/30a). Bede's Death- Song is contained in Cuthbert, 'Epistola De Obitu Bedae', fols 17r/1a-7a. The text belongs to Dobbie's 'Digby' group lacking 'heonengange hwæt his' from lines 3-4.

Physical Description:

Object Description:

Fols ii + 180 + ii; membrane except for paper endleaves. 'Ruling is in crayon, the top/bottom two horizontal lines being extended wide across the outer margin, although on ff. 56, 57, and 63r the bottom three lines and on ff. 64r-66r only the bottom line are so extended. All quires are HFHF, except Quire XXIII, which is HF' (p. 25).

Illuminated initial G 'made up of dragons with blossoms and leaves' (McLachlan, p. 320) in green, red, blue and gold on f. 1r, 'green and gold 'D' on f. 175v; gold 'I' on f. 177r. (Lucas, p. 25). Incipits and explicits in red. 'Capitals in blue, red (sometimes silvered), and green for the capitula on ff. 2v-3v, with chapter numbers in red (and silver). Capitals marking the beginning of books flourished in green, blue, and red' (p. 25). 'S' on f. 145r is described by McLachlan as 'typical but rough Bury flat- colour style' and by Alexander as 'arabesque'. Capitals to mark chapter divisions in red, blue and green; names of authorities in red.

There are annotations in English of s.xii 2 on ff. 4r 'weoloc scælle', 'seolas', 'octo hund mile long & twa hund mile brad', 4v 'weolocas', 8v 'munuc', 9v 'half mile amurg'.

Foliation (1958) in pencil excluding paper endleaves.

Catchwords are partially visible after cropping by the binder at the end of Quires VIII (f. 64v) and X (f. 80v) but are insufficiently visible to determine whether they are in the hand of the scribe.

Hand description:

    Number of hands: 1

The hand is 'upright, fine and regular' (McLachlan) and 'has been identified with that of the first hand in Cambridge, Pembroke College 72, vol. 1' (Lucas, p. 24). Old English is 'in the main hand and is distinguished in script from Latin only by the use of þ and Þ' (Ker).

Bound in blind-tooled red/ brown leather ca. 1830; rebacked 1947.

History:

    Origin:

    The manuscript belonged to the Benedictine abbey of St. Edmund at Bury St. Edmunds, Suffolk (Ker). 'On f. 1r there occurs the Bury class-mark 'B.296' (number visible only under UV light), B standing for Beda. It is very possible that the manuscript was written there' (Lucas, p. 24).

    Provenance:

    The manuscript probably left Bury at the dissolution of the monasteries (1539), and the provenence became unknown.

    Acquisition:

    'Roberts and Watson report that the manuscript was proposed inter alia by M. R. James as a copy of 'Beda de Gestis Anglorum' in the catalogue of manuscripts belonging to John Dee (1527-1608), but there is no proof of Dee's ownership' (Lucas, p. 24). The manuscript came to Trinity College, Dublin, via James Ussher (1581-1656), archbishop of Armagh, who acquired several of Dee's manuscripts (Roberts and Watson), but also acquired manuscripts from elsewhere.
