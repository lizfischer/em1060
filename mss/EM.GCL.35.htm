Homilies and lives of saints

Date: s. ximed-xi2

Summary:

A collection of seven folios from bindings containing fragments of homilies and lives of saints, including Ælfric's texts, copied in s. xi1 and ximed. Some items were added later in s. xi2 (See, Ker 1957, Art. 1-5.).

Object Description:

294 mm x 223 mm (dimensions of most - size of leaves)
269 mm x 165 mm (dimensions of all - size of written space)

Collation:

Quires: Seven half-sheets. According to Ker, fols 1, 2 were probably adjacent. He also suggests that 'probably three leaves separated fol. 2 from fol. 3. Two leaves separated fol. 4 from fol. 5 and four leaves separated fol. 5 from fol. 6.' (1957, p. 154).

Note:

The folios have uneven measures. Ruling is visible on the rectos of fols 1, 5 and the versos of fols 2-4, 6. 30 long lines.

Hand description:

Number of hands: 3 from 1060 to 1220
Summary: These fragments are written by at least three hands. Ker notes that fols 1-3 and 7 are copied in a hand of s. xi1; a hand of s. ximed copied fols 4-6v; fol. 6v is completed by another hand of s. xi2; and an addition in the margin of fol. 7v is from s. xi2 (1957, p. 154).
  • Hand: main text
    • Scope: Major
    • Script: Insular Minuscule
    • Ker reference: Ker 117 scribe 1
    • Description: fols 1-3, 7.
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand:
    • a of squarish proportion.
    • e with a raised head in clusters such as æg and æt
    • y: 'the left stroke rises higher than the right' (Ker, 1957).
  • Date: s. xi1
  • Hand: main text
    • Scope: Major
    • Script: Insular Minuscule
    • Ker reference: Ker 117 SC2
    • Description: fols 4r-6v. A round, close-set hand (Ker, 1957, p. 154).
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand:
    • e prominently horned, with a raised head in clusters such as æ.
    • descenders: the ends of the descenders curve to the left.
  • Date: s. ximed
  • Hand: main text
    • Scope: Major
    • Script: English Vernacular Minuscule
    • Ker reference: Ker 117 SC3
    • Description: fol. 6v.
    • Summary of the characteristics of the hand:
    • ð curved cross-bared ascender 'which touches, but does not transect, the back'.
  • Date: s. xi2
Hand: addition
  • Scope: Minor
  • Script: English Vernacular Minuscule
  • Description: fol. 7v. An addition in the margin.
  • Date: s. xi2

History:

Origin:

Unknown

Provenance:

Fols 1-3 were found in the Register of Abbot Braunche (1500-10) and Abbot Newton (1510-14). Fols 1, 3 were pastedowns which left offset of writing on the binding boards. Fol. 2 was a flyleaf. Fols 5-6 were pastedowns in another unknown volume (Ker 1957, p. 155).
